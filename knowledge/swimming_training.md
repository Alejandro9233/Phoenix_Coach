# Swimming Training — Beginner to 70.3 Triathlon

## Athlete Context
- Level: Beginner — 2 km in 60 min (~3:00/100m) in 25m pool
- Goal: Comfortable 1.9 km open water swim for 70.3 (target: under 45 min)
- Current CSS (estimated): ~2:45-3:00/100m
- Priority: Technique first, then endurance, then speed

## Critical Swim Speed (CSS) — Your Core Metric

### How to Test CSS
1. Warm up: 10-15 min easy swimming with some drills
2. Swim 400m time trial — fast but sustainable
3. Rest 10-15 min (easy swimming or rest)
4. Swim 200m time trial — fast but sustainable
5. Calculate: CSS pace per 100m = (400m time - 200m time) / 2
6. Re-test every 6-8 weeks to track progress

### CSS Training Zones
| Zone | Pace | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Zone 1 (Recovery) | CSS + 15-20 sec/100m | Easy swimming, recovery |
| Zone 2 (Endurance) | CSS + 5-10 sec/100m | Aerobic base building |
| Zone 3 (Threshold) | CSS pace | Race-pace sustainability |
| Zone 4 (Speed) | CSS - 5-10 sec/100m | Short bursts, anaerobic |

## Essential Drills (Do These Every Session)

### Catch-Up Drill
- One arm stays extended until the other "catches up" before pulling
- Purpose: Improves stroke timing, prevents dropping lead arm
- Common error: Rushing the stroke — be patient

### 6-1-6 Drill (Side Kick Drill)
- Kick on your side for 6 kicks, take 1 stroke, rotate to other side for 6 kicks
- Purpose: Body rotation, balance, streamlining
- Advanced: 6-3-6 (3 strokes between side kicks)

### Fist Drill
- Swim freestyle with closed fists instead of open hands
- Purpose: Forces you to use your forearm as a paddle, improves the "catch" phase
- Do 50m fist, 50m open — feel the difference

### Single-Arm Freestyle
- One arm extended in front, stroke with the other arm only
- Purpose: Isolates mechanics, improves balance and catch
- Alternate arms every 50m

### Tarzan Drill (Water Polo Swim)
- Swim with head up, looking forward
- Purpose: Prepares for open-water sighting, builds neck strength
- Do 25m Tarzan, 25m normal — critical for triathlon racing

## Weekly Session Structure (2-3 sessions/week)

### Session 1: Technique Focus (1500-2000m)
- Warm-up: 200m easy mix (50 free, 50 backstroke)
- Drills: 4 x 50m (catch-up, fist, 6-1-6, single-arm) with 15 sec rest
- Main: 6 x 100m at Zone 2 (CSS + 5-10 sec), focus on form, 15 sec rest
- Cool-down: 200m easy

### Session 2: CSS Threshold (2000-2500m)
- Warm-up: 300m easy with 4 x 25m build
- Pre-set: 4 x 50m drill with 10 sec rest
- Main: 10 x 100m at CSS pace, 15-20 sec rest (THE money set)
- Cool-down: 200m easy

### Session 3: Endurance (2000-2500m)
- Warm-up: 200m easy
- Main: 4 x 400m at Zone 2 (steady), 30 sec rest between
- Include sighting practice: lift eyes every 8 strokes
- Cool-down: 200m easy backstroke

## Progression Plan (12 months to 70.3)

### Months 1-3: Build Comfort (2x/week)
- Focus almost entirely on drills and easy swimming
- Goal: Swim 1500m continuously at any pace without stopping
- Work on breathing (bilateral — breathe every 3 strokes)
- Use pull buoy to reduce kick fatigue while learning form

### Months 4-6: Build Endurance (2-3x/week)
- Introduce CSS test and CSS-paced intervals
- Build to 2500m per session
- Start open-water swimming if possible (once per month minimum)
- Work on sighting and drafting skills

### Months 7-10: Race-Specific (3x/week)
- Increase CSS interval volume (work up to 15 x 100m)
- Introduce race-pace 1500m continuous swims
- Practice mass start simulation (hard first 200m, then settle)
- Open water swims every 2 weeks

### Months 11-12: Taper & Race
- Reduce volume 30-40%
- Maintain CSS touches
- Final open-water rehearsal 2 weeks before race
- Race strategy: go out controlled, negative split if possible

## Key Beginner Mistakes to Avoid
- Swimming too fast on every lap — most improvement comes from technique at slow speeds
- Skipping drills — drills are where you actually learn to swim faster
- Death-gripping the water — relax your hands, fingers slightly apart
- Holding breath — exhale continuously underwater, inhale quickly when turning head
- Looking forward while swimming — keep head neutral, look at pool bottom
- Not using a tempo trainer or pace clock — swimming without pacing is unproductive
