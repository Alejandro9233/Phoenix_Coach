# Workout Library — Complete Prescription Menu

## How the AI Coach Uses This File
When prescribing a workout, select from this library based on:
1. The athlete's current phase (foundation/base/build/peak/taper)
2. Today's sport focus (determined by weekly schedule)
3. Recovery status (HRV, RHR, TIB/TSB, RPE)
4. Recent training history (avoid 3 hard days in a row)

## RUNNING WORKOUTS

### Easy Run (E)
- Zone: Z1-Z2 (or Daniels E pace)
- Duration: 30-60 min
- Purpose: Aerobic base, recovery, volume accumulation
- When to prescribe: Recovery days, day after hard sessions, most days
- Frequency: 2-4x/week (majority of running should be here)

### Long Run
- Zone: Z1-Z2 (easy, controlled)
- Duration: 60-150 min (build gradually)
- Purpose: Endurance base, fat oxidation, mental toughness
- When to prescribe: Weekends, once per week
- Progression: Add 10-15 min per week, deload every 4th week
- Can include 20-30 min of M-pace in the final third (build phase only)

### Tempo Run (T)
- Warm-up: 10-15 min Z1-Z2
- Main: 20-40 min continuous at T pace (Daniels) or Z3-Z4 (Friel)
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Improve lactate clearance, race-pace comfort
- When to prescribe: 1x/week during base/build phase
- NOT when fatigued — check HRV and TIB first

### Cruise Intervals
- Warm-up: 15 min Z1-Z2
- Main: 4-5 x 6-8 min at T pace, 1 min jog recovery between
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Same as tempo but with recovery breaks for pacing consistency
- When to prescribe: Alternative to tempo run, good for beginners at threshold training

### VO2max Intervals (I)
- Warm-up: 15 min Z1-Z2
- Main: 5-6 x 3-5 min at I pace (Z5b HR), equal time recovery
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Improve maximum oxygen uptake
- When to prescribe: Build/peak phase, athlete must be well-recovered
- NEVER prescribe if TIB < -15 or HRV is declining

### Repetitions (R)
- Warm-up: 15 min Z1-Z2 + dynamic stretches
- Main: 8-10 x 200-400m at R pace, full recovery (jog back, ~90 sec)
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Running economy, speed, neuromuscular activation
- When to prescribe: 1x/week, can add after easy runs as strides

### Strides/Openers
- Duration: Within an easy run, do 6-8 x 20-30 sec accelerations
- Recovery: 60-90 sec easy jog between
- Purpose: Neuromuscular activation, maintain speed without fatigue
- When to prescribe: After easy runs (2-3x/week), 2 days before race

### Marathon Pace Long Run
- Warm-up: 15-20 min easy
- Main: 10-15 km at goal marathon pace
- Cool-down: 10-15 min easy
- Purpose: Race-specific preparation, pacing practice
- When to prescribe: Build phase only, 1x every 2 weeks, on long run day

### Progressive Run
- Duration: 40-60 min total
- Structure: Start at Z1, gradually increase pace every 10 min, finish last 10 min at Z3-Z4
- Purpose: Teach pace control, finish strong, practice negative splitting
- When to prescribe: Build phase, good alternative to tempo run

## CYCLING WORKOUTS (Static Bike, HR-Based)

### Endurance Ride
- Zone: Z2 (143-157 bpm)
- Duration: 60-150 min
- Purpose: Aerobic base, fat oxidation
- When to prescribe: 1-2x/week, backbone of cycling training

### Sweet Spot Intervals
- Warm-up: 10 min Z1-Z2
- Main: 3 x 15-20 min at high Z3/low Z4 (162-170 bpm), 5 min recovery
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Maximum aerobic adaptation per unit of fatigue
- When to prescribe: Base/build phase, 1x/week

### Threshold Intervals
- Warm-up: 15 min progressive
- Main: 3-4 x 10 min at Z4 (166-175 bpm), 5 min Z2 recovery
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Raise lactate threshold
- When to prescribe: Build/peak phase, 1x/week

### VO2max Intervals (Bike)
- Warm-up: 15 min Z1-Z2
- Main: 5 x 4 min at Z5b (181-188 bpm), 4 min Z1 recovery
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Maximum aerobic power
- When to prescribe: Peak phase only, well-recovered

### Sprint Intervals
- Warm-up: 15 min Z1-Z2
- Main: 8 x 30 sec MAX effort, 2 min Z1 recovery
- Cool-down: 10 min Z1
- Purpose: Neuromuscular power, leg speed
- When to prescribe: Supplement to endurance rides, 1x every 2 weeks

## SWIMMING WORKOUTS

### Technique Session
- Total: 1500-2000m
- Warm-up: 200m easy mix
- Drills: 4 x 50m (catch-up, fist, 6-1-6, single-arm), 15 sec rest
- Main: 6 x 100m at Zone 2 (CSS + 5-10s), focus on form, 15 sec rest
- Cool-down: 200m easy
- When to prescribe: Every week, especially for beginners

### CSS Threshold
- Total: 2000-2500m
- Warm-up: 300m easy + 4 x 25m build
- Pre-set: 4 x 50m drill, 10 sec rest
- Main: 10 x 100m at CSS pace, 15-20 sec rest
- Cool-down: 200m easy
- Purpose: Raise sustainable race pace
- When to prescribe: 1x/week — this is the key swim session

### Endurance Swim
- Total: 2000-2500m
- Warm-up: 200m easy
- Main: 4 x 400m at Zone 2 (steady), 30 sec rest
- Include sighting practice every 8 strokes
- Cool-down: 200m easy backstroke
- When to prescribe: 1x/week, builds aerobic capacity

### Sprint Set
- Total: 1500-2000m
- Warm-up: 300m easy
- Main: 8 x 50m FAST (Zone 4), 30 sec rest + 4 x 100m at CSS, 15 sec rest
- Cool-down: 200m easy
- Purpose: Speed and power development
- When to prescribe: Build/peak phase only

## BRICK SESSIONS

### Standard Brick (Build Phase)
- Bike: 60-90 min at Z2-Z3
- Immediate transition (practice T2!)
- Run: 15-25 min at Z2
- Purpose: Train bike-to-run transition, adapt legs to running off the bike
- When: 1x/week during build/peak phase
- NEVER when athlete is fatigued (check TIB first)

### Race Simulation Brick (Peak Phase)
- Bike: 90 min at race effort (Z2-Z3)
- T2 practice (full gear change)
- Run: 30-40 min, first 10 min easy, then race pace
- Practice race nutrition during the bike
- When: 2-3x in the 6 weeks before race

## REST DAY OPTIONS

### Complete Rest
- No exercise at all
- When: After a race, during deload weeks, when RPE is consistently high

### Active Recovery
- Very easy Z1 swimming: 20-30 min, focus on drills
- OR easy walk: 20-30 min
- Purpose: Blood flow without training stress
- When: Between hard sessions, on "easier" rest days
