# Periodization — Marathon (7 months) → 70.3 Triathlon (~12 months)

## Athlete Context
- Marathon: ~7 months away (December 2026)
- 70.3 Half Ironman: ~12 months away (May/June 2027)
- Current running volume: ~20 km/week (max was 115 km/month — injury occurred)
- Swimming: Beginner (2 km in 60 min)
- Cycling: Static bike only (no power meter, no outdoor bike)
- Strength: Hypertrophy-focused, flexible on days
- Strategy: Use swim + bike as CROSS-TRAINING during marathon block — builds tri fitness without extra running impact

## 12-Month Phase Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-2 — May/June 2026)
**Priority: Build consistency, fix swim technique, establish aerobic base**
| Sport | Sessions/Week | Volume | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Running | 3x | 20→28 km/week | Easy runs, build gradually (10% rule) |
| Swimming | 2x | 2-3 km/session | Technique drills, CSS test, build comfort |
| Cycling | 2x | 60-75 min/session | Z2 endurance on static bike |
| Strength | 3x | Push/Pull/Legs | Hypertrophy focus — this is the best window for muscle building |

- Intensity: 90% Zone 1-2, 10% Zone 3
- Total: ~7-8 hours/week
- Key goals: Swim 1500m continuously, run 30 min easy without pain

### Phase 2: Marathon Base (Months 3-4 — July/August 2026)
**Priority: Build running volume safely, maintain swim/bike as cross-training**
| Sport | Sessions/Week | Volume | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Running | 4x | 28→40 km/week | Add long run (60→90 min), introduce 1 tempo/week |
| Swimming | 2x | 3-4 km/session | CSS intervals, building endurance |
| Cycling | 2x | 75-90 min/session | Z2-Z3, sweet spot intervals 1x |
| Strength | 3x | Push/Pull/Legs | Hypertrophy but watch total fatigue — reduce sets if running suffers |

- Intensity: 80% Zone 1-2, 20% Zone 3-4
- Total: ~9-10 hours/week
- Key goals: Long run hits 90 min, consistent 40 km/week without pain
- CRITICAL: 3:1 build/recovery cycle — every 4th week reduce all volume 20-25%
- Cross-training benefit: Swim and bike build aerobic capacity without running impact stress

### Phase 3: Marathon Build (Months 5-6 — September/October 2026)
**Priority: Marathon-specific fitness, race-pace work, long run progression**
| Sport | Sessions/Week | Volume | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Running | 4-5x | 40→55 km/week | Long run to 28-32 km, tempo + M-pace work |
| Swimming | 2x | 3-4 km/session | Maintain CSS work — don't increase volume |
| Cycling | 1-2x | 60-75 min/session | Easy Z2 only — recovery cross-training |
| Strength | 2-3x | Reduce volume | Maintain strength, shift from hypertrophy to maintenance |

- Intensity: 80% Zone 1-2, 20% Zone 3-5
- Total: ~10-12 hours/week
- Key workouts:
  - Long run with M-pace finish (last 6-10 km at marathon goal pace)
  - Cruise intervals: 4-5 x 8 min at T pace
  - MP long run: 15-18 km at goal marathon pace
- Strength adjustment: 2 sets instead of 3-4, keep compound lifts, drop accessories
- Swim/bike are RECOVERY tools here — easy effort only on hard running days

### Phase 4: Marathon Taper + Race (Month 7 — November/December 2026)
**Priority: Taper, race, recover**
- Weeks -3 to -1: Standard marathon taper (see tapering.md)
- Reduce running volume 50-60% by race week
- Swim and bike: keep 1-2 easy sessions/week for blood flow
- Strength: 1 session/week max, stop all lifting 7-10 days before race
- RACE WEEK: trust the taper, execute the plan

### Phase 5: Recovery + Tri Transition (Month 8 — January 2027)
**Priority: Recover from marathon, pivot to balanced triathlon training**
- Week 1-2: Very easy swimming and walking only. No running.
- Week 3: Reintroduce easy cycling (30-40 min Z1) and short easy runs (20 min)
- Week 4: Resume structured training at 60-70% of pre-marathon volume
- Strength: Can resume hypertrophy focus — this is another good muscle-building window
- Mental: Shift focus to triathlon goals. Set swim and bike targets.
- Key shift: Running is no longer #1 priority — balanced 3-sport training begins

### Phase 6: Tri Base (Months 9-10 — February/March 2027)
**Priority: Balance all 3 sports, build bike endurance, improve swim speed**
| Sport | Sessions/Week | Volume | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Running | 3x | 30-40 km/week | Maintain marathon fitness, 1 quality session |
| Swimming | 3x | 5-7 km/week | CSS threshold sets, build to race distance (1.9 km continuous) |
| Cycling | 3x | 3-5 hours/week | Build toward 90 km ride, introduce threshold intervals |
| Strength | 2-3x | PPL or Upper/Lower | Shift toward race-prep: less hypertrophy volume, maintain compounds |

- Introduce brick sessions: 1x/week (bike → run)
- Total: ~10-12 hours/week
- Practice T1 (swim-to-bike) and T2 (bike-to-run) transitions

### Phase 7: Tri Build + Peak (Month 11 — April 2027)
**Priority: Race-specific fitness, race simulation**
| Sport | Sessions/Week | Volume | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Running | 3x | 35-45 km/week | Race-pace segments, brick runs |
| Swimming | 3x | 5-7 km/week | Open water practice, race-pace 1900m |
| Cycling | 3-4x | 5-7 hours/week | Long rides to 2.5-3h, threshold intervals |
| Strength | 2x | Maintenance only | 2 sets, heavy compounds, no accessories |

- Peak weekly volume: 12-14 hours
- Race simulation bricks: 2-3x this month
- Practice race nutrition on every long bike + brick

### Phase 8: Tri Taper + 70.3 Race (Month 12 — May/June 2027)
- See tapering.md for 70.3 taper protocol
- 2-week taper: reduce volume 40-50%
- Final race simulation brick: 3 weeks before race
- Stop strength training 10 days before race
- RACE!

## The 80/20 Intensity Distribution (Fitzgerald/Seiler)

### The Rule
- 80% of all training volume should be at LOW intensity (Zone 1-2, below VT1)
- 20% of training volume at MODERATE-TO-HIGH intensity (Zone 3+)
- This is by TIME, not by session count

### Why It Works (Science)
- Training below VT1 (first ventilatory threshold) causes minimal autonomic nervous system stress
- Allows high training volume without excessive fatigue
- High-intensity work provides the stimulus, low-intensity work provides the adaptation window
- "Threshold training" (Zone 3) accumulates fatigue without proportional benefit — the "grey zone"
- Elite athletes across all endurance sports consistently show this 80/20 pattern

### Practical Application
- Easy runs should be TRULY easy — conversational, ego-free
- Do not "moderate" your easy days — this is the #1 mistake amateur athletes make
- If you have 5 sessions in a week: 4 easy, 1 hard
- If you have 7 sessions: 5-6 easy, 1-2 hard

## The 3:1 Build/Recovery Pattern
- Week 1: Build (increase volume ~10%)
- Week 2: Build (increase volume ~10%)
- Week 3: Build (increase volume ~10%)
- Week 4: Recovery (reduce volume 20-30%, maintain some intensity touches)
- Repeat cycle

### Recovery Week Rules
- Volume drops 20-30% across all sports
- Keep 1-2 short quality sessions (strides, openers) to avoid detraining
- Extra sleep and nutrition focus
- Address any nagging soreness or tightness
- Mental break — enjoyment sessions only

## Cross-Training Philosophy
Swimming and cycling during the marathon block serve TRIPLE duty:
1. **Aerobic fitness**: Build cardiovascular capacity without running impact
2. **Triathlon preparation**: Develop sport-specific skills months ahead of the 70.3
3. **Injury prevention**: Replace "junk" running miles with non-impact cardio

This approach is ideal for an athlete with a running injury history (max 115 km/month before injury). Rather than pushing running volume dangerously high, supplement with swim/bike to achieve the same total aerobic training load with lower injury risk.

## Key Periodization Rules
- Never increase volume AND intensity in the same week
- After illness or missed week: restart at 70% of previous volume
- After injury layoff >7 days: restart at 50%, rebuild over 3-4 weeks
- One sport can be in "build" while another is in "maintain" — don't build everything at once
- Monitor load ratio (Acute:Chronic): keep between 0.8-1.3
- ALWAYS respect the 3:1 cycle — deload weeks are not optional
