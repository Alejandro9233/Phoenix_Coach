# Short Distance Training — 5k & 10k Race Preparation

## How the AI Coach Uses This File
When the athlete's race distance is 5k or 10k, retrieve this knowledge alongside periodization.md for distance-appropriate coaching decisions. Shorter races demand fundamentally different training emphasis compared to marathons.

---

## 5k Race Physiology

### The Demands
- Duration: 14-25 min for most recreational athletes
- Energy systems: ~95% aerobic, ~5% anaerobic
- Critical limiter: **VO2max ceiling** — the 5k is run at 95-100% of VO2max
- Lactate clearance rate is secondary to VO2max power

### Training Implications
- **Volume is NOT the key lever** — a 5k runner needs 25-35 km/week peak, not 55+ km
- **Speed and VO2max are the key levers** — interval training at I-pace (3-5 min reps) is the primary stimulus
- **Running economy matters hugely** — strides, hill sprints, and repetitions improve neuromuscular efficiency
- **Long runs should cap at 12-15 km** — no benefit from marathon-length long runs
- **Strength training should include plyometrics** — explosive power transfers directly to 5k speed

### Key 5k Workouts (by phase)
| Phase | Primary Quality Sessions |
|---|---|
| Foundation | Strides (6-8 x 20s after easy runs), Easy aerobic volume |
| Speed Build | VO2max intervals (5x3-5 min at I-pace), Repetitions (8-10 x 200-400m at R-pace), Tempo (20 min at T-pace) |
| Taper | Strides/openers only, reduce volume 40-50% |

### 5k Taper Protocol
- **Duration**: 7-10 days (NOT the 3-week marathon taper)
- **Volume reduction**: 40-50% from peak volume
- **Intensity**: Maintain short speed touches — 4-6 strides 2 days before race
- **Last quality session**: 5 days before race (e.g., 4x800m at race pace)
- **Last 48 hours**: Very easy 20-min shakeout run or complete rest

## 10k Race Physiology

### The Demands
- Duration: 30-55 min for most recreational athletes
- Energy systems: ~97% aerobic, ~3% anaerobic
- Critical limiter: **Lactate threshold** — the 10k is run at 88-92% of VO2max
- Sustained threshold-pace running is the race-specific skill

### Training Implications
- **Moderate volume is important** — 30-40 km/week peak, with a long run of 16-20 km
- **Threshold training is the key lever** — cruise intervals and tempo runs at T-pace
- **VO2max intervals are important but secondary** — support the ceiling above threshold
- **Long runs provide meaningful aerobic returns** unlike for 5k
- **Strength shifts from hypertrophy to maintenance + power** as race approaches

### Key 10k Workouts (by phase)
| Phase | Primary Quality Sessions |
|---|---|
| Foundation | Strides, Easy aerobic volume, Long run (60-75 min easy) |
| Base | Tempo run (20-30 min at T-pace), Cruise intervals (4-5 x 6-8 min at T-pace), Long run |
| Speed Build | VO2max intervals (5x3 min at I-pace), Cruise intervals, Progressive run, Repetitions |
| Taper | Strides/openers only, reduce volume 40-50% |

### 10k Taper Protocol
- **Duration**: 7-10 days (similar to 5k)
- **Volume reduction**: 40-50% from peak volume
- **Intensity**: Maintain 1-2 short quality sessions (strides, short tempo)
- **Last quality session**: 5 days before race (e.g., 3x1 mile at race pace)
- **Last 48 hours**: Easy 20-min shakeout or rest

## Common Mistakes in Short-Distance Training

### 1. Too Much Volume, Not Enough Speed
- Marathon training mentality leaks into 5k/10k prep
- Running 50+ km/week for a 5k wastes recovery capacity
- **Fix**: Cap weekly volume and invest in 2-3 quality speed sessions

### 2. Long Runs Too Long
- A 30 km long run has zero benefit for a 5k
- The long run should be 2-3x race distance maximum
- **5k long run**: 12-15 km | **10k long run**: 16-20 km

### 3. Wrong Intensity in Quality Sessions
- 5k quality = VO2max intervals (3-5 min reps at 3k-5k pace)
- 10k quality = Threshold intervals (6-10 min reps at 10k-15k pace)
- Marathon-pace work is NOT appropriate for 5k/10k prep

### 4. Taper Too Long
- Marathon taper: 2-3 weeks
- 5k/10k taper: 7-10 days maximum
- Fitness fades faster from speed than from endurance

### 5. Neglecting Running Economy
- Strides should be a daily habit (4-6 reps post easy run)
- Hill sprints (6-8 x 10s max effort) build neuromuscular power
- Plyometrics (box jumps, bounding) improve elastic recoil

## Training Week Templates

### 5k Speed Build — Sample Week (5 runs, 30 km total)
| Day | Session | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Rest or easy spin | 0 |
| Tuesday | VO2max intervals: 5 x 4 min at I-pace, 3 min jog recovery | 8 km |
| Wednesday | Easy run + 6 strides | 6 km |
| Thursday | Tempo run: 20 min at T-pace | 7 km |
| Friday | Rest | 0 |
| Saturday | Repetitions: 8 x 300m at R-pace, full recovery | 5 km |
| Sunday | Easy long run (Z1-Z2) | 12 km |

### 10k Speed Build — Sample Week (5 runs, 38 km total)
| Day | Session | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Rest or easy swim | 0 |
| Tuesday | Cruise intervals: 5 x 8 min at T-pace, 90s jog recovery | 10 km |
| Wednesday | Easy run + 6 strides | 7 km |
| Thursday | VO2max intervals: 5 x 3 min at I-pace, 3 min jog recovery | 8 km |
| Friday | Rest | 0 |
| Saturday | Progressive run: 40 min, start Z1, finish at Z3-Z4 | 7 km |
| Sunday | Easy long run (Z1-Z2) | 16 km |

## Daniels Running Paces Reference (for the Coach)

The LLM should use Daniels pace zones when prescribing interval workouts:
- **E (Easy)**: Conversational, Zone 1-2, 60-75% VO2max
- **M (Marathon)**: Controlled, Zone 3, ~80% VO2max — NOT relevant for 5k/10k
- **T (Threshold)**: Comfortably hard, Zone 3-4, ~88% VO2max — KEY for 10k
- **I (Interval/VO2max)**: Hard, Zone 5, ~95-100% VO2max — KEY for 5k
- **R (Repetition)**: Very fast but short, supramaximal — neuromuscular economy
