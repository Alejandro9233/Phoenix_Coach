# Tapering & Race Preparation

## Sources: Iñigo Mujika (taper research), Joe Friel (Triathlete's Training Bible)

## Marathon Taper Protocol

### 3-Week Taper (Recommended for Marathon)
- **Week -3**: Reduce volume 20-25%, maintain 2 quality sessions
- **Week -2**: Reduce volume 35-40%, 1 quality session (short tempo or race-pace segments)
- **Week -1**: Reduce volume 50-60%, 1 short session with strides
- **Day -2**: 20-30 min easy + 4-6 x 20 sec strides (openers)
- **Day -1**: Complete rest or 15 min very easy walk/jog
- **Race Day**: Execute the plan, trust the taper

### Key Marathon Taper Rules
- The taper "works" because fitness decays slowly (weeks) while fatigue drops fast (days)
- Reduce VOLUME, NOT intensity — keep short, sharp touches to stay activated
- Maintain training FREQUENCY — keep your daily rhythm, just shorten sessions
- If you feel sluggish mid-taper: this is NORMAL ("taper tantrums") — don't panic-train
- Extra sleep: aim for 8-9 hours per night in the final week

## 70.3 Triathlon Taper Protocol

### 2-Week Taper (Standard for 70.3)
- **Week -2**: Reduce total volume 25-30% across all 3 sports
  - Keep 1 short swim at CSS pace
  - Keep 1 moderate bike with 10-15 min at Z3-Z4
  - Keep 1 short run with race-pace segments
  - Maintain 1 strength session (light, maintenance only)
- **Week -1**: Reduce total volume 40-50%
  - Short swim with drills and a few 100m at CSS
  - Short bike with brief Z3 touches
  - Short easy run with strides
  - No strength training
- **Day -2**: Short, easy brick (20 min bike + 10 min run) with a few pace touches
- **Day -1**: Complete rest or 15 min easy swim
- **Race Day**: Race!

## Common Taper Mistakes
1. **Doing too much**: "I feel so fresh, let me do one more session" — DON'T
2. **Panicking about detraining**: Fitness takes 2-3 weeks to decline measurably; fatigue drops in 3-5 days
3. **Changing routine**: Stick to normal sleep/eat/train patterns, just less volume
4. **Ignoring niggles**: A small ache in taper week = see a physio NOW, not on race morning
5. **Trying new gear/nutrition**: NOTHING new on race day — race with what you've trained with

## Race Week Nutrition

### Carb Loading (3 days before race)
- Increase carb intake to 8-10 g per kg bodyweight
- Reduce fiber to avoid GI issues
- Stay well-hydrated but don't over-drink
- Familiar foods only — no experimenting

### Pre-Race Meal (2-3 hours before start)
- Familiar, carb-heavy, low-fiber, moderate protein
- Examples: oatmeal + banana, toast + peanut butter, rice + egg
- 500-750 ml water or sports drink
- Avoid: dairy, high-fat, high-fiber, anything new

### During Race (70.3)
- Swim: nothing (hydrate before start)
- Bike (90 km): 60-90g carbs/hour (gels, sports drink), 500-750 ml fluid/hour
- Run (21.1 km): 30-60g carbs/hour (gels at aid stations), water at every station
- Practice this EXACT nutrition plan in training on long brick sessions

## Post-Race Recovery
| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Race Day | Celebrate, eat well, hydrate |
| Day 1 | Complete rest, gentle walking only |
| Day 2-3 | Very easy swimming (active recovery) |
| Day 4-5 | Easy bike (Z1 only, 30-40 min) |
| Day 6-7 | Easy short run (20-30 min Z1) |
| Week 2 | Gradually reintroduce normal training at 60-70% volume |
| Week 3 | Return to normal training structure |

After a marathon: take at least 7-10 days very easy before resuming structured training. After a 70.3: take 10-14 days. After a full Ironman: take 2-3 weeks minimum.
