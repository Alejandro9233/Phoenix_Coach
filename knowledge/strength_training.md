# Strength Training — Hypertrophy + Triathlon Concurrent Training

## Athlete Context
- Goal: Build muscle mass (hypertrophy) while training for marathon + 70.3 triathlon
- Flexible on days per week
- Current sports: running, swimming, cycling, gym (training sessions show up as most frequent activity)

## The Core Challenge: Hypertrophy + Endurance
Building muscle while doing significant endurance training requires careful management of the "interference effect" — the tendency for endurance work to blunt hypertrophy signals. The research shows this is manageable with proper programming.

### Key Principles (Research-Backed)
1. Separate strength and endurance sessions by minimum 6-8 hours (ideally different days)
2. If same day is unavoidable: do strength FIRST, endurance SECOND
3. Running creates more interference than cycling or swimming (more muscle damage)
4. Nutrition is the #1 factor — you MUST eat enough protein and total calories
5. Sleep 7-9 hours — this is where muscle protein synthesis peaks
6. Periodize: emphasize hypertrophy when endurance volume is low (off-season/base)

## Programming Strategy by Training Phase

### Off-Season / Base Phase (Hypertrophy Priority)
- Strength: 3-4 sessions/week (Push/Pull/Legs or Upper/Lower split)
- Endurance: Lower volume — maintain aerobic base with easy sessions
- Sets/reps: 3-4 sets x 8-12 reps (classic hypertrophy range)
- Progressive overload: increase weight or reps each week
- Caloric intake: slight surplus (+200-300 cal) to support muscle growth
- Protein: 1.6-2.2 g per kg bodyweight daily

### Build Phase (Balanced)
- Strength: 3 sessions/week
- Endurance: Increasing volume and some intensity
- Sets/reps: 3 sets x 6-10 reps (heavier, maintain strength)
- Caloric intake: maintenance
- Protein: 1.6-2.0 g per kg bodyweight daily
- Key rule: Do NOT increase both strength volume AND endurance volume at the same time

### Race Prep Phase (Endurance Priority)
- Strength: 2 sessions/week (maintenance)
- Endurance: Peak volume and race-specific work
- Sets/reps: 2-3 sets x 6-8 reps (heavy but low volume — maintain, don't build)
- Drop accessory work, keep compound lifts
- Stop all heavy lifting 7-10 days before race

## Recommended Training Split

### Option A: 3-Day Split (Push/Pull/Legs)
Best for: balancing with 6-7 endurance sessions per week

| Day | Focus | Key Exercises |
|---|---|---|
| Push | Chest, shoulders, triceps | Bench press, overhead press, dips, lateral raises, tricep pushdowns |
| Pull | Back, biceps, rear delts | Deadlifts, pull-ups/lat pulldown, barbell rows, face pulls, curls |
| Legs | Quads, hams, glutes, calves | Squats, Romanian deadlifts, lunges, leg press, calf raises |

### Option B: 4-Day Upper/Lower Split
Best for: off-season when endurance volume is lower

| Day | Focus | Key Exercises |
|---|---|---|
| Upper A | Horizontal push/pull | Bench press, barbell rows, overhead press, face pulls |
| Lower A | Quad-dominant | Squats, lunges, leg extensions, calf raises |
| Upper B | Vertical push/pull | Overhead press, pull-ups, incline DB press, lateral raises |
| Lower B | Hip-dominant | Romanian deadlifts, hip thrusts, step-ups, hamstring curls |

## Compound Lifts — The Non-Negotiables
These exercises provide the most benefit for both muscle growth AND athletic performance:
- **Squat** (any variation): Quad, glute, core strength — improves cycling power and running economy
- **Deadlift** (conventional or Romanian): Posterior chain — critical for running power and injury prevention
- **Bench Press**: Upper body pushing — contributes to swim pull power
- **Pull-ups / Lat Pulldown**: Upper back and lats — essential for swimming catch phase
- **Overhead Press**: Shoulder stability — helps maintain posture in late-race fatigue
- **Rows**: Back thickness and posture — counteracts the forward-hunched cycling position

## Managing Fatigue
- If RPE on easy runs exceeds 5/10 → reduce strength volume next week
- If lifts are stagnating for 3+ weeks → check if endurance volume increased too fast
- If HRV drops >10% for 3+ days → take a deload (reduce BOTH strength and endurance)
- Every 4th week: deload both strength (reduce volume 30-40%) and endurance (reduce 20-25%)

## Nutrition Essentials for Concurrent Training
- Post-strength workout: 30-40g protein + 50-80g carbs within 30 minutes
- Post-endurance session: focus on carb replenishment (30-60g carbs + 20-30g protein)
- Daily protein target: 1.6-2.2 g/kg spread across 4-5 meals
- Creatine: 3-5g daily — safe, well-researched, helps both strength and repeated sprint performance
- Hydration: minimum 2-3 liters daily, more on heavy training days
